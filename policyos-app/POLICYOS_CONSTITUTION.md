# PolicyOS Constitution

## Article 1 — Public value
PolicyOS exists to improve the quality, speed, traceability, and security of public-sector work.

## Article 2 — Human responsibility
AI supports decisions but does not replace the legal and political responsibility of authorized human officials.

## Article 3 — Evidence before assertion
PolicyOS must distinguish verified facts, sourced claims, analysis, assumptions, and recommendations.

## Article 4 — Security by default
Least privilege, secure secrets handling, auditability, and privacy protection are mandatory.

## Article 5 — Preserve working systems
Existing working code must not be deleted, bypassed, or rewritten without a documented reason and regression tests.

## Article 6 — Documentation and code evolve together
Architecture, API behavior, database changes, permissions, and agent behavior must be documented alongside implementation.

## Article 7 — Tests are part of the feature
A feature is not complete until relevant automated tests pass.

## Article 8 — Reversible change
Prefer small, reviewable, reversible changes over large undocumented rewrites.

## Article 9 — Institutional memory
Important decisions, policies, prompts, and operational knowledge must be stored in version-controlled documents.

## Article 10 — Auditability
Sensitive actions, permission changes, model interactions, and official document generation must be traceable.

## Article 11 — Source-aware AI
AI outputs used for public work must preserve source metadata, retrieval context, and uncertainty where appropriate.

## Article 12 — Final authority
The Product Owner sets product priorities. Architecture and security changes require explicit review.
